Kickass live-acts within Propellerhead Reason

About the project

This project enables you to play live-acts in Propellerheads Reason. I created this project, because it's not really obvious how to do live shows within Reason. It can be great if you play in a band and use it as an instrument, but when you need to mix between 10 tracks and build up, tweak each track, then it becomes ticky to set everything up correctly.

That's cool, but what is it?

This is a simple .reason project file, that you can customize to your music. You need to build your tracks into a combinator. Inside a combinator, there's a Kong or other looping devices that you can turn on/off with a MIDI controller. You can change focus and it will keep playing.

Download now (v0.1 alpha)

Features & requirements

You can

  • Forward CV messages to the currently selected combinator
  • Two channel output
  • Select on each combinator which channel to route the audio
  • Select on each combinator which group of MIDI knobs to listen to
  • Turn REX loops on/off in each combinator and keep them playing tempo synced by sending MIDI notes

You will need

  • A MIDI controller with at least 8 knobs or faders
  • Some time to convert your tracks to combinators
  • Reason 8 is ideal, because it's easier to switch focus of combinators, but any Reason version is good that supports Rack Extensions

Required Rack Extensions

  • A/B Audio & CV Switch (Free, download)
  • Blamsoft Polymodular Audio merger (Free, download)
  • Blamsoft Polymodular CV splitter (Free, download)
  • Morfin XF Crossfader (Free, download)
  • CV8x4 CV Generator (Paid, download)
